The Southie factor: Narrow streets, limited parking, tight building access, and active neighbors mean construction cleanup in South Boston requires a crew that knows how to work efficiently in constrained spaces — without disrupting the block. 🏗️ The Three Phases of Post-Construction Cleaning

Professional post-construction cleaning is not a single event. It is a structured, multi-phase process designed to systematically eliminate every trace of construction activity. Blue Brick follows industry-standard protocols across three distinct phases:

1. Rough Clean

The rough clean begins as soon as major construction is complete but before finish work is installed. This phase focuses on removing large debris — drywall scraps, lumber offcuts, packaging materials, nails, and dust accumulation on rough surfaces. We clear the site so finish carpenters, painters, and flooring installers can work in a clean environment. This step is critical because construction dust left behind during the rough phase becomes embedded under finish materials, causing long-term problems.

2. Light Clean

After finish work is installed — countertops, fixtures, hardware, appliances — the light clean addresses the secondary layer of dust and debris these installations generate. We wipe down all surfaces, vacuum HVAC registers and ductwork openings, clean window frames and tracks, and remove adhesive residue, paint overspray, and caulking smears. Every cabinet interior, every shelf, every closet is detailed.

3. Final Clean

The final clean is the white-glove pass that transforms a construction site into a move-in ready property. This is where Blue Brick's standards separate us from general cleaning services. We polish all glass — windows, mirrors, shower enclosures — until they are streak-free. We detail every appliance interior and exterior. We hand-clean light fixtures, outlet covers, and switch plates. We vacuum and mop all flooring with products matched to the material. The result is a property that looks, feels, and smells like it has never seen a day of construction.
