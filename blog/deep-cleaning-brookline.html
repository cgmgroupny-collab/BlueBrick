What Our Deep Cleaning Service Includes

A Blue Brick deep clean is not a quick wipe-down. It is a room-by-room, surface-by-surface process designed to eliminate accumulated dirt, bacteria, and allergens that degrade your home's appearance and air quality over time. Here is what you can expect.

Kitchen Deep Clean

The kitchen is where grease, food residue, and bacteria concentrate most aggressively. Our deep cleaning process includes degreasing range hoods, exhaust fans, and backsplashes. We clean inside and behind the oven, refrigerator, microwave, and dishwasher. Countertops, cabinet fronts, and drawer interiors are wiped down with appropriate products. Sinks are scrubbed and sanitized. Floor grout is hand-cleaned. Every handle, knob, and switchplate receives attention. In Brookline's older kitchens with custom tile work and butcher block surfaces, we use specialized methods that protect these premium materials.

Bathroom Deep Clean

Bathrooms accumulate soap scum, hard water stains, mildew, and bacteria faster than any other room. We deep clean tile and grout on walls and floors, descale showerheads and faucets, scrub inside toilet bowls and behind toilet bases, clean exhaust vents, wipe down medicine cabinets inside and out, and polish mirrors and glass to a streak-free finish. Brookline's many older homes feature vintage tilework and porcelain fixtures that require careful product selection — we know which cleaners are safe for these surfaces and which will cause permanent damage.

Living Areas and Bedrooms

Deep cleaning living spaces means going beyond vacuuming and dusting. We clean baseboards, door frames, window sills, and light fixtures. Ceiling fan blades are wiped down. Upholstery is vacuumed with HEPA-filtered equipment to extract embedded allergens. Behind and beneath furniture gets thorough attention. Air vents and registers are cleaned to improve indoor air quality. Window tracks are detailed. Every surface that routine cleaning overlooks is addressed.

Floors

Brookline homes feature a range of flooring materials — original hardwood in historic properties, engineered wood in newer condos, marble and tile in entryways and bathrooms, and high-quality carpet in bedrooms and dens. Our crews are trained in the proper cleaning methods for each material. Hardwood floors are cleaned with pH-neutral solutions that protect the finish. Tile grout is scrubbed by hand. Carpets are deep-vacuumed using commercial-grade HEPA equipment. We never use products or techniques that compromise your flooring investment.

Every Blue Brick deep clean is customized to your home's specific surfaces, layout, and condition. We do not use a one-size-fits-all checklist — we assess your property and tailor our approach accordingly.

The Spring Cleaning Advantage

New England winters are hard on homes. Months of sealed windows, running heating systems, road salt tracked indoors, and reduced ventilation create conditions that leave every surface in your home carrying a winter's worth of accumulated buildup. Brookline residents who invest in a professional spring deep clean start the warmer months with a home that looks, smells, and feels genuinely refreshed.

A spring deep clean goes beyond aesthetics. Removing dust from HVAC vents and returns before switching from heat to air conditioning improves system efficiency and reduces allergen circulation. Cleaning windows inside and out brings in more natural light. Scrubbing winter grime from entryway tile and hallway floors eliminates the salt residue that deteriorates these surfaces over time. It is one of the most effective investments a Brookline homeowner can make in their property's long-term condition.

Deep Cleaning vs. Regular Cleaning: What Is the Difference?

Regular cleaning maintains surface-level tidiness — vacuuming visible floors, wiping counters, cleaning toilets and sinks, and general dusting. It keeps your home looking presentable between deeper sessions. A deep clean, by contrast, addresses the accumulated grime that regular cleaning misses.

Think of regular cleaning as maintenance and deep cleaning as restoration. Regular cleaning preserves the status quo. Deep cleaning resets your home to a higher baseline. Most Brookline households benefit from a professional deep clean every three to six months, supplemented by regular maintenance cleaning in between. If you have pets, allergies, young children, or a household with high foot traffic, quarterly deep cleaning provides the best results.
