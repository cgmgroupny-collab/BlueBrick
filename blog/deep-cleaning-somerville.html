💡 Why Somerville Homes Need Professional Deep Cleaning

Somerville is one of the most densely populated cities in New England, and its housing stock reflects over a century of continuous use. A professional deep cleaning strips all of that away. It is the reset button for your living space — a thorough, systematic cleaning that addresses every surface, corner, and crevice in your home. Whether you are preparing for a move, recovering from a long winter, or simply maintaining the standard your home deserves, deep cleaning delivers visible, measurable results that routine tidying cannot match.

📋 What Professional Deep Cleaning Includes

Deep cleaning is not the same as regular maintenance cleaning. It is a comprehensive, top-to-bottom process that reaches the areas most people overlook week after week. Here is what Blue Brick's deep cleaning service covers in Somerville homes.

🍳 Kitchen Deep Cleaning

The kitchen is where grease, food residue, and bacteria accumulate fastest. Our deep cleaning covers the interior and exterior of all appliances — inside the oven, behind the refrigerator, underneath the microwave, and along the range hood and exhaust filter. We degrease backsplashes, scrub sink basins and faucets, clean inside cabinets and drawers, wipe down countertops and edges, and detail the areas around and behind appliances where crumbs and grime collect unnoticed. In Somerville's older kitchens, we pay particular attention to the tight spaces and dated layouts common in triple-decker units.

🚿 Bathroom Deep Cleaning

Bathrooms require aggressive sanitization. We scrub tile and grout lines, remove hard water deposits and soap scum from shower walls and glass doors, clean behind and around toilets, sanitize vanity surfaces, polish fixtures, and detail exhaust fan covers. Many Somerville apartments have original tile work that traps mildew in grout lines over the years. Our cleaning process targets that buildup specifically, restoring grout to a visibly cleaner state.

🛏️ Living Areas and Bedrooms

Deep cleaning in living spaces includes thorough dusting of all surfaces — shelving, window sills, door frames, baseboards, crown molding, ceiling fan blades, and light fixtures. We vacuum under and behind all furniture, clean interior windows and tracks, wipe down switchplates and door handles, and address cobwebs in ceiling corners. For hardwood floors, which are standard in most Somerville homes, we provide detailed mopping that cleans without damaging the finish.

✨ ✨ ✨ Triple-Deckers: Somerville's Signature Cleaning Challenge

The triple-decker is Somerville's defining residential structure. These three-story, three-unit buildings were built throughout the early 1900s and remain the backbone of the city's housing stock. They present unique deep cleaning considerations.

  • Plaster walls and ceilings accumulate fine dust that settles on every horizontal surface and requires careful dusting techniques to avoid damage.
  • Radiator heating systems collect dust, pet dander, and debris between fins over an entire heating season. Deep cleaning includes detailed radiator cleaning to improve both air quality and heat efficiency.
  • Original hardwood floors need cleaning methods that remove grime without stripping protective finishes or leaving moisture damage.
  • Older windows with rope-and-pulley systems have deep tracks and sills that trap dirt and insect debris season after season.
  • Compact layouts mean furniture is placed tightly against walls and into corners, creating hard-to-reach zones that only professional equipment can effectively clean.

Blue Brick's teams understand these properties because we clean them every week across Somerville. We know the quirks, the materials, and the techniques that deliver results without causing damage.

Whether your Somerville home is a century-old triple-decker or a brand-new Assembly Row apartment, Blue Brick delivers deep cleaning calibrated to your property's specific materials and needs.

📅 When to Schedule a Deep Cleaning

There is no wrong time to deep clean your Somerville home, but certain moments deliver the most value.

  • Before or after a move. Moving is the single best time to deep clean. An empty unit allows full access to every surface, and the results are dramatically better than cleaning around furniture.
  • After winter. Boston-area winters seal homes tight for months. By spring, dust, allergens, and stale air have accumulated significantly. A deep clean in March or April resets indoor air quality for the warmer months ahead.
  • Before hosting. If you are preparing for guests, a holiday gathering, or a significant event, a deep clean ensures your home looks and feels its absolute best.
  • Quarterly maintenance. Many Blue Brick clients schedule deep cleanings every three to four months to prevent buildup and maintain a consistently high standard.
  • After renovation or repairs. Even minor home projects generate dust that infiltrates every room. A post-project deep clean removes what your contractor left behind.
