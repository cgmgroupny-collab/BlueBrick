What a Professional Turnover Clean Includes

A proper turnover clean goes well beyond a quick wipe-down and vacuum. Every surface a guest might see, touch, or open needs to be addressed. Blue Brick's turnover cleaning process covers every room systematically.

Kitchen Reset

The kitchen is where guests notice shortcuts immediately. Our team cleans all countertops, sanitizes the sink and faucet, wipes down the exterior and interior of the microwave, cleans stovetop burners and drip pans, wipes the oven door, empties and sanitizes the refrigerator, runs and empties the dishwasher if needed, cleans the coffee maker, and ensures all dishes, utensils, and cookware are clean and properly stowed. We check for any items previous guests may have left behind and dispose of all trash, replacing liners in every bin.

Bathroom Sanitization

Bathrooms receive a full sanitization. Toilets are cleaned inside and out, including the base and behind the tank. Showers and tubs are scrubbed, glass doors are squeegeed and polished, tile grout is checked for buildup, and vanities and mirrors are cleaned streak-free. We restock toilet paper, replace used towels with fresh linens, and arrange toiletries according to your staging preferences. Drains are checked for hair, and floors are mopped with a disinfecting solution.

Linen Change and Bed Staging

All bed linens, pillowcases, and mattress protectors are stripped and replaced with freshly laundered sets. Beds are made to a hotel-standard presentation, with tight corners and neatly arranged pillows. If you provide linen service through Blue Brick, we handle the laundering as well. Throw blankets and decorative pillows are inspected for stains or pet hair and replaced or refreshed as needed.

Living Areas and Guest-Ready Details

All hard surfaces are dusted and wiped. Upholstery is checked for debris and vacuumed. Floors are vacuumed and mopped throughout. Light switches, door handles, remote controls, and thermostats are sanitized. Windows are checked for fingerprints. We reset the space exactly as it should appear in your listing photos: blinds at the right angle, throw pillows in position, welcome materials set out, and the overall impression that no one has been there before.

How to Maintain 5-Star Cleanliness Reviews

Consistently earning 5-star cleanliness ratings is not about one perfect clean. It is about building a reliable system that never drops below the standard. Here is what separates hosts who maintain a 5.0 cleanliness score from those who fluctuate.

  • Use the same professional team every time. Consistency matters more than anything. When the same crew cleans your property repeatedly, they learn its quirks, know where dust collects, and deliver uniform results. Switching between random cleaners or handling it yourself when your regular person cancels is where standards slip.
  • Invest in quality linens and replace them on schedule. Stained sheets, pilled towels, and yellowed pillowcases undermine even the most thorough clean. Professional-grade white linens show guests that cleanliness is taken seriously. Replace towels every 6 months and sheets annually.
  • Eliminate odors at the source. Air fresheners mask problems. Professional cleaning addresses the source: deep-cleaning upholstery, treating garbage disposal drains, washing curtains quarterly, and ensuring the HVAC filter is clean. A property that smells truly neutral reads as clean.
  • Stage deliberately. A clean property that looks cluttered will still receive lower cleanliness scores. Minimize countertop items, keep closets organized, and ensure every surface has visual breathing room. Guests associate open space with cleanliness.
  • Act on feedback immediately. If a guest mentions a hair in the bathroom or dust on a shelf, treat it as a systems failure and address the root cause. Do not dismiss isolated complaints. Patterns start with a single missed detail.

The Between-Guest Cleaning Checklist

Whether you clean yourself or hire a professional team, every turnover should cover these essentials. Use this as a minimum standard.

  1. Strip and remake all beds with freshly laundered linens. Inspect mattress protectors for stains.
  2. Replace all used towels including hand towels, bath mats, and kitchen towels.
  3. Sanitize all bathrooms completely: toilet, shower, sink, mirror, floor, and restock supplies.
  4. Reset the kitchen to a fully clean state: appliances, counters, sink, trash, and dishware.
  5. Vacuum and mop all floors including under furniture edges and in corners.
  6. Dust all surfaces including shelves, nightstands, TV screens, window sills, and ceiling fan blades.
  7. Sanitize high-touch surfaces: light switches, door handles, remote controls, keys, lockbox, thermostat.
  8. Empty all trash cans and replace liners throughout the property.
  9. Check for previous guest items in drawers, closets, under beds, and between couch cushions. Follow your lost-and-found protocol.
  10. Inspect and restock amenities: toilet paper, soap, shampoo, coffee, dish soap, sponge, trash bags.
  11. Do a final walkthrough comparing each room against your listing photos. Everything should match.

Pricing Factors for Airbnb Turnover Cleaning

Turnover cleaning pricing depends on several variables. Understanding these factors helps you budget accurately and avoid surprises.

  • Property size. A studio or one-bedroom turnover takes less time and fewer supplies than a two-bedroom or larger unit. Pricing scales with the number of rooms, bathrooms, and total square footage.
  • Linen service. If Blue Brick provides laundered linens as part of the turnover, this is factored into the per-clean rate. If you supply your own linens on-site, the cost is lower.
  • Turnover frequency. Hosts with regular weekly or bi-weekly turnovers benefit from recurring scheduling rates. One-off or irregular cleans may carry a slightly higher per-visit rate.
  • Same-day turnaround. Standard turnovers with a full day of lead time are priced differently from tight same-day windows that require precise scheduling and dedicated team allocation.
  • Deep cleaning add-ons. Periodic deep cleans, such as oven interior cleaning, inside-of-cabinet wipedowns, baseboard detailing, or window washing, are quoted separately and scheduled between bookings when time allows.
