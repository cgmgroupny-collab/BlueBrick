Key Takeaways

  • Boston landlords can legally deduct cleaning costs from your security deposit if the unit is not returned in clean condition
  • A professional move-out clean covers areas routinely missed during regular cleaning
  • Blue Brick's move-out checklist is designed to meet Greater Boston landlord standards
  • Book 2–3 days before your move-out date to ensure availability

Security deposits in Boston are significant. With average rents among the highest in the country, a first-and-last deposit can represent $4,000–$8,000 or more. Losing even a portion of that to cleaning deductions is money you cannot afford to leave behind.

Massachusetts law allows landlords to deduct the cost of cleaning from your security deposit if the unit is returned in worse condition than when you moved in. The standard is "reasonable wear and tear" — and most landlords interpret that strictly. A professional move-out cleaning service is the most reliable way to protect your deposit.

What Blue Brick's Move-Out Clean Covers

  • Kitchen — oven interior and racks, refrigerator inside and out, microwave, all cabinets and drawers, countertops, sink and faucet, floor scrubbed
  • Bathrooms — toilet inside and out, tub and shower scrubbed, sink and vanity, mirrors, grout lines, floor mopped
  • All rooms — baseboards wiped, windowsills cleaned, light switches and outlets wiped, closet interiors dusted and vacuumed
  • Floors — hardwood polished or carpet vacuumed and spot-treated, entryway and mudroom scrubbed
  • Walls and doors — scuff marks removed, door frames and handles wiped

Boston tip: Pay particular attention to the oven, refrigerator, and bathroom grout — these are the three areas Boston landlords most commonly cite when making deductions.

When to Book Your Move-Out Clean

Book your move-out cleaning after all furniture and personal items are removed, and at least 24–48 hours before your final walkthrough with the landlord. This gives you time to address any items the landlord flags during the walkthrough.
