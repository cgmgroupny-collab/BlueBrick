04

Logo Usage

Consistent logo application protects brand recognition. Follow these rules across all media and materials.

Minimum Size
The stacked logo should never appear smaller than 80px wide on screen or 25mm wide in print. The horizontal lockup minimum is 120px wide on screen or 40mm in print. The profile icon can render down to 32px.
Clear Space
Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the brick icon on all four sides of the logo. No text, graphics, or visual elements may encroach into this zone.
Background Rules
Use the white-background versions on light surfaces (#F0F2F5 or lighter). Use the dark-background versions on Midnight Navy, Deep Navy, or any surface darker than 50% gray. Never place the logo on busy photography without a solid or semi-transparent overlay.
Prohibited Use
Do not rotate, skew, stretch, or distort the logo. Do not change the logo colors. Do not add drop shadows, outlines, gradients, or effects. Do not rearrange the lockup elements. Do not recreate the logo in alternative typefaces.

05

Brand Voice

Blue Brick communicates with the confidence of a company that does the work and lets results speak. Every piece of copy should feel direct, capable, and premium.

Professional
Speak with authority. Use precise language. No slang, no casual abbreviations. Address property managers and homeowners as equals who expect competence.
Trustworthy
Lead with facts and results. Reference insurance, experience, and track record. Be specific about what we deliver. Underpromise, overdeliver.
Premium
Position every service as high-end. We clean Boston's finest properties, not "houses." Use language that reflects the caliber of our clientele and work.
Masculine
Strong, direct, no-nonsense. Construction cleanup is hard, physical work. The tone should reflect the weight and rigor behind the service, not soften it.
What We Don't Do
  • No sparkle or glitter imagery
  • No playful or cutesy language
  • No feminine visual elements
  • No filler words or fluff copy
  • No stock-photo-style enthusiasm
  • No exclamation marks in headlines
  • No pastel or soft color deviations
  • No rounded, bubbly design elements
